Terms You Need to Know

Stalking
Pursuing deer on foot with a rifle — the UK term for deer hunting.
Open Season
Legal period when stalking a species/sex is permitted.
Close Season
Protected period for breeding & rearing young — stalking prohibited.
Stag / Hind
Male / female Red & Sika deer.
Buck / Doe
Male / female Fallow, Roe, Chinese Water & Muntjac deer.

Legal & Ethical Notes

Scotland, 21 Oct 2023: the close season for all male deer was abolished. Male deer can legally be stalked year-round, though many estates keep traditional summer/autumn cull dates for herd management.

Muntjac: breed year-round so have no close season anywhere in the UK. BASC & the British Deer Society advise culling only immature animals, bucks, or heavily pregnant does — avoid does with dependent young.

Roe Deer & Chinese Water Deer are not present in Northern Ireland; their listed dates apply to England & Wales only.

Red/Sika hybrids follow the same open season dates as pure Red and Sika deer.

Permission & licensing: stalking requires explicit written permission from the landowner or shooting rights holder, plus compliance with minimum calibre/energy rules under the Deer Act 1991 (England & Wales) or the Deer (Scotland) Act 1996. Most estates require a DSC1 or DSC2 certificate.
